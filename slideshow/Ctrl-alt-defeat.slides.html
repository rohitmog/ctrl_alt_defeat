What is the question that your team set out to answer?¶

Our team set out to answer "Which major groups should we target marketing efforts on?"¶

We started by hypothesizing that alumni are more willing to buy football tickets than the general public.¶

We had a hunch alumni might be more willing to buy football tickets than the general public:¶

According to the NY Times, in 2017, Michigan graduates had a median income of $68,700.¶

In contrast, according to US Census ACS survey, Michigan residents had a median income of $30,488.¶

The main takeway from this comparison is that Michigan alumni have a lot more disposable income.¶

In the next section, we investigate our hypothesis:¶

Since we had sales data for the 2015-2019 football seasons, alumni data, and a unique ID for each buyer/alumni, we merge these two data sets by ID below:¶

Conclusion and Recommendations to Maximize Marketing Dollars¶

Based on number of ticket sales and revenue generated, alumni in the 50-70 year old age range are the top supporters of the Michigan football program.¶

Heavily target these folks.¶

Historically, more recent graduates don't buy as many tickets and don't have as much disposable income as older graduates.¶

They are more connected to campus and likely receive advertising already - older graduates would benefit more from advertising.¶

If Michigan football wants to reach the majority of the 50-70 year old alumni demographic, they must use multi-channel methods to market tickets.¶

This means many advertising efforts should be allocated to physical media such as magazines and postcards that this age demographic views.¶

Lastly, Michigan football should run lots of Facebook ads targeting the 50-70 year old alumni demographic.¶

Facebook provides the ability to only allocate advertising resources to extremely specific demographics, allowing for cost effective marketing.¶
